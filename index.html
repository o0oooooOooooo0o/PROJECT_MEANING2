How much should you trust the meaning you're making right now?

Reading the eyes before the eyes.

Before you act, react, decide, send the message, chase the urge, quit the thing, or turn a passing mood into a verdict about yourself — this instrument estimates the condition of the machine that is currently producing your sense of what's true. It does not tell you what to think. It tells you how much weight your thinking can safely carry today.

“Meaning sticks to state. State becomes the eyes before the eyes — the atmosphere through which perception is filtered before thought finishes forming itself. If you do not understand the machinery producing meaning, you will mistake its outputs for truth. You will call dysregulation reality.”

— Project Meaning, founding text
Tier One · The Level

How activated is the body?

Borrowed from clinical neuroscience: arousal (how charged or shut-down you are) is separate from the content of your mind. Too high and too low both distort. The trustworthy zone is settled-but-alert — your window. This is measured on its own dial, because a flooded mind and a foggy mind lie in opposite directions.

Tier Two · The Content

How clear is the meaning-making?

Eight readings of the same moment: can you read your body, feel without being run, notice an urge without obeying it, see a thought as a thought, hold more than one story, stay present, and act as the author of your next step. Together they set the Trust Coefficient — your dial of authority.

What this is not. Not a medical, neurological, or psychiatric test. Not for coma, brain injury, psychosis, mania, seizure risk, intoxication, or crisis. It is a mirror for ordinary waking life — a snapshot rated by the very mind it is assessing. If your safety or someone else's is at risk, set this down and reach a real person or emergency support.

Before you act — is this a good moment to…

A verdict, not an order. Hold means the state can't carry it yet; Caution means make it small, slow, or reversible; Go means you're clear enough — still sanity-check once.

The chain interceptor

When an urge or a thought feels like an order, slow the four links that turn a body-signal into an action. You can step out at any link.
→
Link 01 · Sensation
What's in the body

Name it in plain physical words — where it is, what it feels like, how strong, 0–10. Pressure, heat, tightness, hollowness. Just the sensation, not yet the meaning.

→
Link 02 · Salience
What grabs the attention

Notice what the charged body is dragging your focus toward — the person, the screen, the worst-case, the exit. Salience is the state choosing what looks important.

→
Link 03 · Appraisal
The story you jump to

Say it as: “I'm having the thought that…” A thought held this way stops being a fact you must obey and becomes a mental event you can examine.

Link 04 · Action urge
What the body wants to do

Name the urge out loud and rate how believable it feels, 0–100. High believability is the state talking, not proof. You can feel the full pull and still not move.

Then surf it. An urge is not a command; it is a wave. It builds, peaks, and falls on its own — usually inside minutes — whether or not you act. Watch it crest without feeding it. Each time you ride one out, the body learns the wave is survivable, and the next one arrives with less authority.

Shift the state — then read again

Reading is half the work; the other half is changing the weather on purpose. This runs a short, state-matched breathing exercise. Do it, then take a fresh reading and watch the dial move — that before-and-after is the nervous system learning it can shift.
Ready
Want proof a state can move? Run a measured test: it saves a Before reading, you do the shift and update your sliders, then take an After reading and see exactly what changed — and how movable your state is.
